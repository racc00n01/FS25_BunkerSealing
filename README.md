# Bunker Sealing

A mod for **Farming Simulator 25** that adds a **sealing quality** layer to bunker silos. How well you cover the silage before it ferments affects how much product you keep when the bunker is opened—not just for looks.

## What it changes

- After you **compact and cover** the bunker, the game tracks how completely the top is sealed using **bales** and/or **bunker seal tires** placed on the silage surface.
- **Better sealing** means **less silage lost** when fermentation finishes and you open the bunker. **Thin or patchy coverage** means more of your crop is treated as spoilage and disappears.
- While you are near a **still-sealed** bunker (before you open it to unload), a short hint can show your current **seal efficiency** as a percentage. That line is **hidden** once the bunker is **opened** for unloading, because sealing no longer applies.

## Bunker seal tires

- Buy **bunker seal tires** in the **shop** under **Miscellaneous**. They are meant to be placed on the silage like weights and add to your seal coverage (same idea as using bales).

## Settings (optional)

In the **in-game menu**, open **Settings** and find the **Bunker Sealing** section under the game settings. There you can tune difficulty and behaviour to match your taste, for example:

- **How harsh** poor sealing is, and the **maximum possible loss** from bad coverage.
- **Seal difficulty** (easier modes treat bale placement as more effective, so you need fewer bales for a strong seal).
- How much area each **tire** or **bale** counts as covering on the bunker surface.
- A **debug grid** option that draws the seal grid on the bunker (mainly for checking coverage).

Multiplayer games use the **host’s** settings.
